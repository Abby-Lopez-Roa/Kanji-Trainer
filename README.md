# 漢字 Trainer

A flashcard app for drilling kanji from Genki I & II (Lessons 3–21). Works in any browser and can be saved to your phone's home screen like a native app.

**[▶ Open the app](https://Abby-Lopez-Roa.github.io/Kanji-Trainer/kanji-trainer.html)**

---

## Study modes

The app has three flashcard modes, all active by default. You can toggle any of them off in Settings.

| Mode | You see | You recall |
|------|---------|------------|
| Reading → Kanji | Hiragana + English meaning | The kanji |
| Kanji → Reading | Kanji only | The hiragana reading |
| Kanji + Meaning → Reading | Kanji + English meaning | The hiragana reading |

Cards are drawn randomly across all three modes unless you turn some off.

---

## How to use it

1. A card appears showing whatever the current mode prompts you with
2. Think of your answer
3. Tap **Reveal** to see the answer
4. Mark yourself honestly — **Got it** or **Missed it**
5. The deck automatically weights missed cards more heavily, so they come back sooner

There's no typing — it's pure honor-system flashcards, just like Quizlet's card mode.

---

## Scoring

- **XP** — earn 10 XP for every correct answer. Every 100 XP levels you up.
- **Streak** — counts consecutive correct answers across the whole session. Resets on any miss.
- **Session counter** — tracks correct and missed answers for your current session (resets when you close the app).

---

## Settings

**Focus on mistakes** — once you've built up some wrong answers, toggle this on to drill only your weak spots.

**Lesson filter** — defaults to all lessons (L3–L21). Tap individual lessons to include or exclude them. Useful if you want to focus on a specific unit.

---

## Progress

The **Progress tab** shows:
- Total cards reviewed and overall accuracy
- Current and best streak
- A ranked list of your most-missed kanji

Use **Reset all progress** to wipe scores and start fresh. Your settings (active modes, lesson filter) are preserved.

---

## Add to home screen

To use it as a full-screen app on your phone:

**iPhone:** Open in Safari → Share button → "Add to Home Screen"

**Android:** Open in Chrome → three-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen"
